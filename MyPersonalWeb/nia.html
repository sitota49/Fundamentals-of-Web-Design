Visit to NIA

January 21 2019, was the day we visited the NIA foundation's: Joy center for children with autism. My friends and I had initially planned to volunteer this coming summer at the foundation as we wanted to widen our awareness about autism and as we wanted to make an impact on the children's lives. The Civic and Ethical Education assignment initiated us to go earlier than planned. We were well informed and oriented about autism and what the foundation mainly works on. Autism is a developmental disorder of variable severity that is characterised by difficulty in social interaction and communication and by restrictive and repetitive patterns of thoughts and behaviour. Getting informed more about the topic assured us that we would visit very often and contribute to the foundation in whatever way we can.
